Frequently Asked Questions

Why and when should I consult a Pediatrician?

Paediatricians manage the health of your child, including physical, mental health and behavioural issues. They are trained to diagnose and treat childhood illnesses, from minor health problems to serious diseases. Your child needs to see a paediatrician if they are showing any of the below symptoms.


  • -Persistent Cough for more than a Week.
  • -Chest pain
  • -Abdominal pain or Colic
  • -High Fever of 101F not subsiding with medication
  • -Headache
  • -Cold, severe sneezing, sore throat and runny nose.

What are the conditions that our Pediatrician can treat?

ADHD: Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a mental health disorder that can cause above-normal levels of hyperactive and impulsive behaviours. People with ADHD may also have trouble focusing their attention on a single task or sitting still for long periods of time. It is a chronic condition where even parents and care providers need to be counselled.


Dysplasia: It is the presence of abnormal cells within a tissue or organ which is not cancer but can turn into one. Early identification and treatment are necessary to prevent it from becoming malignant.


Sore Throat: Can be a bacterial or viral infection presenting with throat pain, fever, and inability to swallow or drink.


Bronchiectasis: is a disease in which there is permanent enlargement of parts of the airways of the lung. Symptoms typically include a chronic cough with mucus production. Other symptoms include shortness of breath, coughing up blood, and chest pain.


Hernia: It is a bulge near the belly or groin and goes away by the time the child turns 5. In some cases, it persists even after the 5th Year leading to problems for the child.


Food poisoning: Vomiting, diarrhoea, gas, bloating, abdominal pain are some of the symptoms.


Vitamin Deficiency: Vitamin deficiency is the condition of a long-term lack of a vitamin caused by not enough vitamin intake hence needing supplements.
